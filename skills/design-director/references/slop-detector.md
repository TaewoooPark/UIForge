# The slop detector — an adversarial "prove it's AI" judge

The linter catches what's grep-able. This catches what isn't: the *gestalt* of
"this was generated." Run it as a **separate, implementation-blind pass** — ideally
a subagent (Task tool) given **only the rendered screenshot(s)**, not your code or
your reasoning. Self-graders skew positive; an adversary doesn't.

## The brief for the judge

> You are a skeptic whose only job is to **prove this UI was generated by an AI.**
> You are given screenshots of a page. Assume it is guilty. Find the tells and cite
> them with specifics (what, where). Only if you genuinely cannot build a case do
> you return CLEAN.

Give the judge the screenshot(s) — normal **and** `prefers-reduced-motion` — at
desktop width, and this checklist to argue from:

- **Layout:** centered hero + subhead + two buttons + three identical cards? A
  badge above the H1 that says nothing? Everything symmetric, no asymmetry? A big
  empty middle? → *template.*
- **Type:** looks like Inter/Roboto/system? One reflexive italic-serif word in the
  headline? Tiny uppercase tracked label over an oversized heading? → *default.*
- **Color:** purple/indigo/violet anywhere? A gradient headline? Evenly-weighted
  rainbow instead of one dominant + one accent? Grey text on a colored panel? →
  *AI palette.*
- **Surface:** `rounded-2xl` + soft `shadow-lg` cards with a colored top/left
  stripe? Unmotivated glow/neon? Glassmorphism? Over-rounded everything? Cards
  inside cards? Bento-grid-for-its-own-sake? → *the AI card signature.*
- **Copy:** hype words ("unlock/supercharge/seamless/world-class/trusted by
  thousands")? A headline that could be pasted onto any competitor unchanged
  (fails the specificity test)? Emoji as icons/bullets? → *generated copy.*
- **Motion (compare the two screenshots):** does more than one thing obviously
  move? Anything spinning/looping for no reason? Is the reduced-motion frame
  identical/complete, or is content missing?

## The verdict

The judge returns one of:

- **AI-DETECTED** — with a numbered list of tells + where each is. **This fails.**
  Fix every cited tell, then re-render and re-run the judge.
- **CLEAN** — it could not make the case. Only then may you ship.

The bar is not "no BLOCKERs in the linter." The bar is **an adversary given the
pixels cannot prove a machine made it.** That is the whole product.
